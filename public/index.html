Advancing Space Exploration and Communication

Breaking Barriers in Space Communication

In making humanity multiplanetary, we need to advance our space exploration with incremental research and development. In space exploration, a reliable communication architecture is not just important for mission success but is vital for ensuring the safety and operational efficiency of all space activities. Building communication in cislunar space (the space between Earth and the Moon) is a critical step towards our goal. Unlike previous eras, this time the cislunar space communication architecture needs to be far more advanced and easily extendable. However, the lunar environment presents unique challenges. Given the complexity of the cislunar environment, direct communication isn?t always possible due to the distance, obstacles (like the Moon itself), and the movement of spacecraft. Despite all these challenges, communication in cislunar space is an important technology that ensures robust and continuous communication between Earth, spacecraft, lunar surface assets, and beyond.

Empowering Cislunar Communication Through Innovation

In exploring different cislunar communication, we provide research and development tools to simulate cislunar network architecture with high fidelity. We have the expertise of supporting cislunar satellite orbits from different orbital families, as well as the "Manifold" that defines the pathway of space travel between Earth and the Moon. In addition, we provide highly detailed and configurable communication channels between satellites and ground stations. Furthermore, we incorporate the occlusion effect and environmental impact on communication. Our simulation results depict the accurate behavior of cislunar communication, ensuring a realistic approach to advancing space technologies.
