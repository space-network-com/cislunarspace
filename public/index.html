Advancing Space Exploration and Communication

Breaking Barriers in Space Communication

To make humanity multiplanetary, we must advance space exploration through incremental research and development. Reliable communication architecture is vital for mission success, safety, and operational efficiency. Building communication in cislunar space (between Earth and the Moon) is a critical step towards this goal. Unlike previous eras, the cislunar communication architecture must be more advanced and easily extendable. The lunar environment presents unique challenges, and direct communication is not always possible due to distance, obstacles (like the Moon), and spacecraft movement. Despite these challenges, robust and continuous communication between Earth, spacecraft, lunar surface assets, and beyond is essential.

Empowering Cislunar Communication Through Innovation

We provide tools to simulate cislunar network architecture with high fidelity, supporting various cislunar satellite orbits and pathways between Earth and the Moon. Our detailed, configurable communication channels link satellites and ground stations, incorporating occlusion effects and environmental impacts. Our simulations ensure accurate depiction of cislunar communication behavior, advancing space technologies with precision.
